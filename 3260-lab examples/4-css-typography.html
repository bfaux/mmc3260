About Me

I am currently studying at the University of Florida in the College of Journalism and Communications but I originally wanted to be a musical theater actor. I have participated in theater at various venues in my local community for the majority of my life. I studied acting and voice on and off for many years. I was an active performer at the Vam York Theater in Gainesville, Florida for the past six years where I still ocassionally volunteer in other areas.



I love the collaborative nature of theater and the way people can come together, connect to each other and an audience, as well as create art. I love to discuss art and popular culture and survey their impact on society. I often find myself reading The New York Times reviews of Broadway shows and I came to the realization that I was interested in writing critical analysis as well as commenting on the state of contemporary American theater.



With the digital age of social media, podcasts, and blogs everyone has something to say about popular culture and all it's many forms. I realized I wanted to be in a profession where I could give my statement or opinion and potentially have my opinion matter. Although this is not the only aspect of journalism, I find it to be an important one. I strive to be a better writer and storyteller which stems from my experiences in the performing arts. I want to be a part of the ongoing discussion of art and culture in unique and contemporary ways and connect with others through that very discussion.
